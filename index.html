Frequently Asked Questions

Empowering you with authentic facts to make informed medical choices.

What is the difference between IUI and IVF?

IUI (Intrauterine Insemination) is a simple procedure where washed sperm is injected directly into the uterus during ovulation.

IVF (In Vitro Fertilization) is an advanced procedure where the eggs and sperm are fertilized outside the body in a laboratory setting, and the resulting embryos are placed inside the uterus. IVF has significantly higher success rates for complex infertility.

How long does a standard IVF cycle take?

One complete IVF cycle typically takes about 4 to 6 weeks. This includes ovarian stimulation (10-12 days of injections), egg retrieval, lab fertilization, embryo culture (3 to 5 days), and the final embryo transfer. A pregnancy test is taken 14 days after transfer.

What factors affect IVF success rates?

Success depends on multiple variables:
  • Maternal age (the single most critical parameter)
  • Egg and sperm quality
  • Uterine lining receptivity
  • Underlaying causes of infertility (PCOS, fibroids)
